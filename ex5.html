Bears are found largely in northern temperate regions and are widely distributed in the Americas,
Europe, and Asia. They are found farther north than any other mammal.
The Arctic fox is found as far north on land, but the polar bear regularly roams on sea ice hundreds of miles (hundreds of kilometers)
from shore. No bears live in the wild in Africa and Australia. (The koala “bear” of Australia is not really a bear;
it is a marsupial, like kangaroos and opossums.)
The spectacled bear of the South American Andes Mountains is the only species that lives south of the Equator.
Bears live in a wide range of habitats, including mountain and lowland forests, tundra, grasslands, and swamps.
